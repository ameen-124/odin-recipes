Beef Stew

Beef stew is a hearty and comforting dish made by slow-cooking tender chunks of beef with a variety of vegetables and flavorful broth. This classic meal is perfect for chilly days, as it warms you from the inside out. The beef becomes melt-in-your-mouth tender, while the vegetables absorb the rich flavors of the stew. It's a one-pot meal that’s easy to prepare and is often even better the next day!

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups carrots, sliced
  • 2 cups potatoes, diced
  • 1 cup celery, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1 cup frozen peas (added at the end)

Steps

  1. Brown the Beef: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes in batches, browning them on all sides. Remove the browned beef and set aside.
  2. Sauté Onions and Garlic: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  3. Add Broth and Seasonings: Return the browned beef to the pot. Pour in the beef broth and add the tomato paste, thyme, rosemary, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. Add Vegetables: Add the carrots, potatoes, and celery to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it she last 10 minutes of cooking.
  6. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.
  7. Serve: Remove the bay leaf before serving. Ladle the beef stew into bowls and enjoy it hot, ideally with crusty bread or over rice.
